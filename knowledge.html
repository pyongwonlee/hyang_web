ACCUMULATED KNOWLEDGE
2007
ACCUMULATED KNOWLEDGE is my attempt to materialize the process of reading.
During the first semester of the MFA program at the University of Guelph, I typed every text that I read on a computer. The act of typing was simultaneous with the act of reading.

I started typing with the font size 10. As soon as the end of the first page was reached, I stopped my reading and typing. Rather than moving to the second page, I reduced the font size by 1 (from 10 to 9). With the reduced font size, I was able to read and type more until the end of the first page was reached again. I repeated this process (reading/typing and reducing the font size) until the font size became 1. In so doing, all the knowledge and information, obtained from my reading for a semester, was compressed in one page.
